Unit 4: Computer Accessories

Exam Focus: This unit extensively covers input and output devices, their types, and ergonomic considerations. Expect questions requiring you to differentiate between devices, explain their functions, and discuss ergonomic design principles.

4.1 The Input Accessories

Input accessories are peripheral devices that allow users to feed data and instructions into a computer system. They convert human-readable input into a machine-understandable binary format.

Keyboard Devices

The most common text-entry device, allowing users to input characters, numbers, and symbols, and to control computer functions.

  • Application: Text editing, command input, gaming.
  • Types: QWERTY (standard), ergonomic, mechanical, virtual.

Point and Draw Devices

Devices that allow users to control the position of a pointer (cursor) on the screen for selection, navigation, and drawing.

  • Mouse: A hand-held device tracking movement relative to a surface.
  • Trackball: A stationary device with a ball rotated by the user's hand.
  • Touchpad/Trackpad: A pressure-sensitive surface, mainly on laptops.
  • Stylus/Light Pen: Used for drawing or selecting on screens.

Yoke

A specialized input device, typically resembling an aircraft steering wheel or joystick, primarily used for flight simulation and certain driving games.

Data Scanning Devices

Exam Question Alert: Be ready to explain different types of data scanning devices and their applications.

Devices that read text, images, or codes directly from a source document and convert them into digital data.

  • Optical Mark Reader (OMR): Reads marks made with a pencil or pen (e.g., multiple-choice test sheets).
  • Optical Character Recognition (OCR): Scans and converts images of typed, handwritten, or printed text into digitally editable text.
  • Bar Code Reader: Scans barcodes (parallel lines) to input product information.
  • Magnetic Ink Character Recognition (MICR): Reads special magnetic ink characters printed on bank cheques.

Digitizer (Graphics Tablet)

An input device consisting of a flat pad and a stylus, used for drawing or writing, with the image digitized and sent to the computer.

  • Application: Graphic design, architectural drawings, digital art.

Microphone

A device that converts sound waves (voice, music) into electrical audio signals, which are then converted into digital data by a sound card.

  • Application: Voice recording, voice commands, video conferencing.

Electronic Cards Based Devices

Exam Question Alert: Electronic card-based devices can be a short note question. Understand their function and examples.

Devices used to read data stored on cards, typically for security or transaction purposes.

  • Smart Card Readers: Read data from cards containing embedded microchips (e.g., SIM cards, payment cards).
  • Magnetic Stripe Readers: Read data stored on a magnetic stripe (e.g., credit cards, key cards).

Speech Recognition Devices

Exam Question Alert: Be prepared to explain the application areas of speech recognition devices.

Software and hardware systems that enable the computer to identify and respond to the sound of spoken words.

  • Application: Voice control systems (e.g., Siri, Google Assistant), dictation software, accessibility tools.

Vision Based Devices (Digital Camera and Video Input)

Devices that capture visual information (still images or video frames) and convert them into digital data for processing.

  • Digital Camera/Webcam: Captures still or moving images.
  • Image Scanners: Captures high-resolution digital representations of physical documents.

4.2 The Output Accessories

Output accessories are peripheral devices used to display or present processed data (information) from the computer to the user in a human-readable or usable format.

Monitor (Visual Display Unit - VDU)

Exam Question Alert: Expect questions differentiating between types of monitors (e.g., CRT vs. LCD) and discussing softcopy output devices.

The primary output device for displaying visual information (softcopy).

  • Types: CRT (Cathode Ray Tube - older), LCD (Liquid Crystal Display), LED (Light Emitting Diode), OLED (Organic LED).
  • Key Characteristics: Resolution, refresh rate, size, and aspect ratio.

Printer

Exam Question Alert: Differentiating between impact and non-impact printers is a common question.

Produces a physical, hard copy of documents and images.

  • Impact Printers: Strike an inked ribbon against the paper to create an image (e.g., Dot Matrix, Daisy Wheel).
  • Non-Impact Printers: Use ink spray or toner powder without physical contact (e.g., Inkjet, Laser).

Plotter

A specialized output device used to produce large, high-quality graphics, such as architectural blueprints and engineering designs, by drawing lines with a pen.

  • Application: CAD (Computer-Aided Design), map making, large-scale posters.

GPS (Global Positioning System)

While primarily a navigation system, a GPS receiver acts as an output accessory by providing location, velocity, and time information to a computer application.

Projectors

A device that projects a computer's video output onto a large external screen or surface.

  • Application: Presentations, movie viewing, large-group instruction.

Headphones & Speakers

Audio output devices. Headphones are worn over the ears for private listening, while speakers broadcast sound for multiple listeners.

Soundcard/Video Card

These are internal expansion cards essential for enabling audio and video output.

  • Sound Card: Processes audio signals and directs output to speakers/headphones.
  • Video Card (GPU): Processes graphical data and directs output to the monitor.

Voice Response System (Text-to-Speech)

A system that enables a computer to "speak" or generate human-like audio output from text data (Text-to-Speech or TTS).

  • Application: Interactive voice response (IVR) systems, accessibility tools.

Computer Output Microfilm (COM)

A specialized output technology that transfers data from a computer directly onto microfilm or microfiche, offering a compact way to archive large amounts of data.

SGD (Speech Generation Device)

Devices specifically designed to assist individuals who have difficulty speaking (e.g., Text-to-Speech communicators used by people with vocal disabilities).

4.3 Ergonomically Designed Devices

Exam Question Alert: Expect questions on the importance and examples of ergonomically designed devices and how they enhance user efficiency.

Ergonomics is the science of designing and arranging things people use so that the people and things interact most efficiently and safely. In computing, ergonomic design aims to reduce user fatigue, discomfort, and the risk of long-term health issues.

  • Ergonomic Keyboards: Split-design, negative tilt, padded wrist rests to reduce strain on wrists and forearms.
  • Ergonomic Mice: Vertical grip, contoured shape, large scroll wheels to keep the wrist in a neutral handshake position.
  • Adjustable Monitors: Allow for height, tilt, and swivel adjustments to ensure the screen is at eye level, preventing neck strain and reducing reflections.
  • Ergonomic Chairs and Desks: Adjustable height, lumbar support to promote good posture and back health.

Key Ergonomic Guidelines for Computer Users:

  • Posture: Maintain a straight back, feet flat on the floor, and arms parallel to the floor.
  • Monitor Distance: Position the monitor an arm's length away.
  • Breaks: Take frequent short breaks (e.g., every 20-30 minutes) to stretch and rest eyes.
