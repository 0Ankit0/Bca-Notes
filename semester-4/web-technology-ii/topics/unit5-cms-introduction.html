5.1 CMS concept and relevance

A CMS is a platform for managing web content with minimal coding. This unit helps students evaluate when to use CMS over custom coding.

5.2 Popular CMS platforms

WordPress (large ecosystem), Drupal (enterprise flexibility), Joomla (balanced structure). Compare plugin models and admin workflows.

5.3 Benefits and scenarios

Use CMS for blogs, institutional sites, and content-heavy portals where editors manage content frequently.

5.4 CMS vs frameworks

Clarify that Flask/Django/Spring are programming frameworks for custom app logic, while CMS targets rapid content publishing.

Classroom pacing for 3 hours

  1. CMS introduction and architecture (1h)
  2. Comparative demonstration (1h)
  3. Decision matrix activity: CMS vs custom framework (1h)
